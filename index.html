Mutual Funds - Key Points To Consider Before Investing





















Stock Market is a term that evokes a variety of emotions for many people. Many feel that it's an unregulated form of gambling, whereas others consider it the best method of losing money. Some people get feeling of excitement when they trade stocks for hours. Certain people use it to build wealth. The fears that surround stocks have gone down considerably since the beginning of the nineties , and now the majority of people are at ease investing in the market. This article is specifically targeted for Indian investors but the concepts discussed are universal.




The stock market is a process that requires meticulous study, regular review , and swift decision making. Picking a stock that is a little too choosy and keeping yourself informed about the company's progress and the timing of your selling and buying can consume a large portion of the time. That's where it is that the Mutual Fund industry can lend you a assistance. A Mutual Fund is managed by a Fund Manager as well as an analyst team who spend their time studying the stock market and then invest your funds. It helps you avoid all the stress of investment in stocks and provides you with a person to manage your funds.




It is true that the Mutual Fund industry has come far since its inception by India in the 90s. Mutual Funds provide a variety of choices based on your risk-taking profile, allowing you to reap the highest tax-effective returns. But I'd like to remind those who invest in mutual funds requires some effort from your end. If you invest in the wrong fund at the wrong time could ruin your savings. The risks of investing in any class of asset (such as commodities, Gold, or stocks or bonds] can be applied to mutual funds as well. For the investor who is more cautious mutual funds can provide the possibility of exposure for fixed-income instruments by way of fixed maturity plans (FMP)/debt funds in which the money you invest is invested in debt instruments. These funds are more tax-efficient than direct investments in bonds, FDs, etc. I will provide below a few points to keep at heart when investing in mutual funds.




a. If you're looking at investing in the short-term (1-3 year) and you want to get the most tax-efficient return, go to Debt funds/FMPs.  pdq loans

If you are looking to be exposed to stock markets , keep in mind that returns from stocks are only achievable in the long-term because markets generally are seen-saws that have an upward tendency in the long run. This means that you could be required to stay for longer than five years. Don't check the value of your NAV(Net Asset Value) every day and be elated or sad because of the fluctuating value.

This assures that the Mutual Fund has the flexibility to absorb a loss should one or two companies they invested in gets into trouble.




D. Always remember that a previous performances are not a guide to future performance. Look for performers who have a consistent performance.


e. Choose a the New Fund Offer (NFO) only in times of a major economic downturn, since this allows the fund to invest in stocks at a lower price. For debt funds, opt for NFOs once interest rates are reaching their peak. Don't invest in an NFO if you are attracted by the clever ad in the press. The majority of NFOs concentrate on the taste of the season to lure you to invest in commodities, Green Energy, Emerging markets etc].Some might be a bit stale and some may die in a natural way. So exercise abundant caution.




F. The ideal time to begin SIPs SIP occurs when the market is beginning to show downward trends. The most likely time to be in a get scared and stop in an SIP occurs when the stock market is plunged to a steep decline. This is actually the time that the most successful investors are rubbing their hands with excitement. This is why you should raise the SIP amount in the event that the market is down, and when the market recovers, you can return to the regular amount. Establish a baseline and set an objective that you can meet - e.g. for every 100 points of decline in Nifty index, increase your SIP to Rs. 1000 and decrease exposure in the same manner when the market recovers.




g. Do not expect to earn extraordinary returns. If you are looking at a long-term basis, mutual funds provide annual returns of 12-15 percent.




H. Do a review at least once every year, and then look up the sectors you believe have peaked.




i. It is suggested to invest in an SIP in an index fund or exchange traded funds (ETF). An index fund invests its funds in companies that make up the index. In the case of an index, for instance, if the fund is based on Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) Sensex which is a BSE Sensex index, it will invest its funds in companies that form the index. The NAV is a proxy for that index. BSE Sensex. This fund will always yield an income that is closely correlated to the performance of the market. This is a safe way to protect yourself from fluctuations in the the price of shares of a sector or company. Stock exchanges will swiftly remove a company from the index in the event that it is underperforming, and your fund will do exactly the similar.

j. Do not mix an insurance product that is invested in stocks, with mutual funds. They are completely distinct products. They are expensive and offer less than mutual funds.


Mutual funds are a great option for those who don't possess the energy or energy to put in the effort to make a successful stock selection. They provide investors with the opportunity to be exposed to various sectors and asset classes depending on risk tolerance and, if picked wisely, could give extremely satisfactory returns to boost the wealth of an investor.
